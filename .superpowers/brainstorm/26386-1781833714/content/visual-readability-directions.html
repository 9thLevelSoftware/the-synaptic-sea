Which visual readability direction gets us out of the rut?

We are not choosing another proof artifact. We are choosing what the next in-engine work should make the actual game feel like.

entry → spine → goal

A. Navigation-first

Make the route readable: clear floors, portals, blockers, ramp, destination, and player orientation. Minimal dressing.

mood / lighting / derelict identity

B. Atmosphere-first

Make it feel like Sargasso: lighting, grime, biomatter, silhouettes, room moods. Navigation improves indirectly.

turn debug markers into game props

C. Marker-to-prop conversion

Replace labels/colored blocks with functional props: supply cache, breaker panel, med terminal, reactor console, obstruction, ramp cue.

My recommendation

  • Start with A + C: navigation clarity plus marker-to-prop conversion.
  • Keep atmosphere limited to lighting and a few silhouettes until the space reads.

What to avoid

  • Do not make another HTML/mockup deliverable.
  • Do not add more objectives before the existing scene reads as a place.
